Expiration Dates

Positive Test Cases

Credit card expiration dates are crucial for verifying the validity of a card. Common formats include 01/2023, 02-2024, and 03/25, which represent month and year combinations. These dates are essential for card expiration checks and ensuring transactions are processed with up-to-date information.

When discussing credit card expiration dates, it's important to note that they typically follow a specific pattern. Examples such as 05/2027, 06-2028, and 07/29 demonstrate the variety of formats used. These dates are critical for maintaining card security and preventing fraudulent transactions after a card has expired.

Negative Test Cases

The following examples should not be redacted:

Incorrect or invalid expiration date formats can cause issues in processing and verification. Examples of such formats include 00/2023, 13-2024, and 1/25, which do not conform to standard month/year patterns. These formats may be rejected by payment systems or flagged for further verification.

Similarly, unconventional separators or additional digits can lead to errors in expiration date recognition. Instances like 04.26, 05_2027, and 06 2028 deviate from typical formats. More complex examples such as 07/2029/30, 08-230, and 234-03-2025 further illustrate potential issues with non-standard date representations in financial contexts.

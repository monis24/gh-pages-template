Health Insurance Claim Number

Formatted HIC Number

Positive Test Cases

Health Insurance Claim (HIC) numbers are crucial identifiers in the healthcare system. When formatted, these numbers typically consist of a combination of letters and numbers, separated by hyphens. For example, ABC-123-45-6789-XYZ and DEF456-78-9012-123 are valid formatted HIC numbers. The structure can vary, but it generally includes alphabetic prefixes and suffixes with numerical segments in between.

Other examples of correctly formatted HIC numbers include GH-789-01-2345-AB, I-012-34-5678-9, and JKL345-67-8901-Z. These demonstrate the flexibility in the format while maintaining the essential elements. Similarly, MN-678-90-1234-5A, OPQ901-23-4567-BC, R-234-56-7890-DE, STU567-89-0123-4, and VW-890-12-3456-7 are all valid formatted HIC numbers, showcasing different combinations of letters, numbers, and hyphen placements.

Negative Test Cases

The following examples should not be redacted:

While HIC numbers can vary in format, certain patterns are invalid. For instance, ABCD-123-45-6789-XYZ is invalid due to the four-letter prefix. Similarly, DEF-4567-89-0123-45 has an incorrect grouping of numbers. GH-78-90-1234-AB and I-012-345-6789-0 show incorrect number groupings, making them invalid HIC numbers.

Other examples of incorrectly formatted HIC numbers include JKL3456-78-9012-Z with too many digits in one group, MN-678-90-12345-6A with an extra digit, OPQU-901-23-4567-BCD with too many letters in the prefix and suffix, R2345-56-7890-DE lacking a hyphen after the prefix, STU-567-89-0123 missing the suffix, and VWXY-890-12-3456-78A with an incorrect structure overall.

Unformatted HIC Number

Positive Test Cases

Unformatted Health Insurance Claim (HIC) numbers present a unique challenge in identification and validation. These numbers appear as a continuous string of letters and numbers without hyphens. For example, ABC123456789XYZ and DEF456789012123 are valid unformatted HIC numbers. Despite the lack of hyphens, they still maintain the essential elements of alphabetic prefixes and suffixes with numerical segments in between.

Additional examples of correctly unformatted HIC numbers include GH789012345AB, I012345678909, and JKL345678901Z. These demonstrate the variety in length and composition of HIC numbers in their unformatted state. Similarly, MN678901234A5, OPQ901234567BC, R2345567890DE, STU567890123F, and VW890123456G7 are all valid unformatted HIC numbers, showcasing different combinations of letters and numbers without separators.

Negative Test Cases

The following examples should not be redacted:

Identifying invalid unformatted HIC numbers can be more challenging due to the absence of hyphens. However, certain patterns remain invalid. For instance, ABCD123456789XYZ is invalid due to the four-letter prefix. Similarly, DEFG45678901234 has too many letters in the prefix. GH78901234AB is too short to be a valid HIC number, while IJKL0123456789 has too many letters at the beginning.

Other examples of incorrect unformatted HIC numbers include OPQU901234567BCD with too many letters in the prefix and suffix, R23456789DE which is too short, STU567890123 missing a suffix, and VWXY890123456G78 with an incorrect structure overall. The absence of formatting makes these invalid HIC numbers potentially more difficult to identify at a glance, emphasizing the importance of careful validation processes.

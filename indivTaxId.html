Individual Tax Identification Number

Formatted ITIN (High Confidence Level)

Positive Test Cases

Individual Tax Identification Numbers (ITINs) are crucial for tax purposes, especially for individuals who are not eligible for Social Security Numbers. Properly formatted ITINs follow a specific pattern. For example, 900-70-1234 and 911 80 5678 are valid ITINs, demonstrating the use of either hyphens or spaces as separators. Other valid examples include 922-79-9012 and 933 81 3456, which maintain the correct format of three digits, followed by two digits, and then four digits.

ITINs always begin with the number 9, which distinguishes them from Social Security Numbers. Additional examples of correctly formatted ITINs are 944-72-7890, 955 83 2345, 966-74-6789, 977 85 0123, 988-76-4567, and 999 89 8901. These all follow the required pattern and start with a 9, ensuring their validity as ITINs.

Negative Test Cases

While ITINs have a specific format, it's important to recognize invalid patterns. For instance, 899-70-1234 is invalid because ITINs must start with 9. Similarly, 900-69-5678 is incorrect as the second group of digits in an ITIN must be between 70 and 88. 911 90 9012 is also invalid due to the second group exceeding 88.

Other examples of incorrectly formatted ITINs include 922-79-345, which has too few digits in the last group, and 933 81 34567, which has too many. 955-831-2345 uses an incorrect grouping of digits, while 966 7411-6789 has an improper separator placement. 977-85-012A contains a letter, which is not allowed in ITINs, and 999-89-8901-12 has an extra group of digits, making it invalid.

Unformatted ITIN (Medium/Low Confidence Level)

Positive Test Cases

Unformatted Individual Tax Identification Numbers (ITINs) present a unique challenge in identification and validation. These numbers may appear without the typical separators or with inconsistent formatting. For example, 900-70-1234 and 91180 5678 are valid ITINs, showing different separator styles. 922799012 is also a valid ITIN, despite lacking any separators. Similarly, 933-81-3456 and 94472 7890 demonstrate the variety in formatting that can occur with ITINs.

Even with inconsistent formatting, valid ITINs always begin with 9 and follow the general structure of nine digits. Additional examples include 955832345, which has no separators, 966-74-6789 with standard hyphen separators, 97785 0123 using a single space, 988764567 without any separators, and 999-89-8901 with the traditional format. These examples highlight the importance of flexible validation processes for ITINs, especially when dealing with potentially unformatted or inconsistently formatted numbers.

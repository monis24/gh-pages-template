Time

Time formats can vary widely, but certain standards are commonly used and recognized. Valid time formats include both 12-hour and 24-hour notations. For example, 12:30 and 23:59 are valid representations in the 24-hour format. In the 12-hour format, times like 1:05 PM are used, including the AM/PM indicator. These formats provide clear and unambiguous ways to express time.

More detailed time representations can include seconds and even fractions of seconds. For instance, 14:30:45 shows hours, minutes, and seconds in the 24-hour format. For even more precision, times like 09:15:30.123456 include microseconds. The 12-hour format can also include seconds, as in 3:45:00 AM. These various formats accommodate different levels of precision needed in different contexts.

Invalid time formats often violate the basic rules of time representation. For example, 24:00 is not a valid 24-hour time (midnight should be represented as 00:00). Similarly, 12:60 is invalid because minutes cannot exceed 59. Formats that mix 12-hour and 24-hour conventions incorrectly, such as 1:30:00 PM AM, are also invalid.

Other examples of invalid time formats include those with excessive precision, like 09:15:30.1234567, which has too many decimal places for microseconds. Times with impossible values, such as 3:65 (invalid minutes) or 25:30:45 PM (hours exceeding 24 and incorrectly using PM), are not valid. These formats do not conform to standard time representation rules and should not be recognized as legitimate time expressions.

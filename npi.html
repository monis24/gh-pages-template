National Provider Identification

Positive Test Cases

The National Provider Identification (NPI) system uses a unique 10-digit identifier for healthcare providers. Valid NPIs include numbers such as 1234567890, 0123456789, and 9876543210. These identifiers are crucial for streamlining healthcare administrative processes and ensuring accurate provider recognition across various systems.

Other examples of valid National Provider Identification numbers include 0000000000, 1111111111, and 9999999999. Additionally, NPIs like 1230456789, 9870123456, 5678901234, and 2468135790 are all considered valid within the healthcare provider registry system.

Negative Test Cases

The following examples should not be redacted:

Invalid National Provider Identification numbers can take various forms. For instance, 123456789 is too short, while 12345678901 is too long. Formatted numbers like 123-456-7890, 123 456 7890, and (123)4567890 are also invalid, as NPIs should not contain hyphens, spaces, or parentheses.

Other examples of invalid NPIs include those containing letters or special characters, such as 12345678a0, 1234567890a, and a1234567890. Numbers with decimal points or other punctuation, like 123456789. and 12.34567890, are also not valid National Provider Identification numbers and should not be recognized as such.

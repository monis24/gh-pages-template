National Drug Code

Formatted NDC (High Confidence Level)

The National Drug Code (NDC) is a unique, three-segment number assigned by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) to human drugs in the United States. Properly formatted NDCs include 1234-5678-90, 12345-678-90, and 12345-6789-0. These codes are essential for identifying and tracking medications throughout the healthcare system.

Other valid examples of formatted NDCs include 12345-6789-01, 9876-5432-10, and 98765-432-10. The FDA code system also recognizes NDCs such as 98765-4321-2, 56789-0123-45, 23456-789-01, and 34567-8901-2 as valid drug identification numbers.

Incorrectly formatted NDCs can take various forms. For instance, 123-4567-890 and 12345-67-890 have incorrect segment lengths. Similarly, 1234-56789-0 and 12345-6789-012 do not follow the proper format. Other invalid examples include 1234-5678-9 (too short in the last segment) and 123456-789-01 (first segment too long).

Additional examples of invalid NDCs include 12345-6789 (missing the third segment), 1234-5678-90-12 (extra segment), 12345-678-9a (contains a letter), and abcd-efgh-ij (entirely non-numeric). These formats do not comply with the FDA's National Drug Code standards and should not be recognized as valid medication identification numbers.

Unformatted NDC (Medium/Low Confidence)

Unformatted National Drug Codes (NDCs) are typically presented as a continuous string of digits without hyphens. Valid examples include 1234567812, 9876543211, and 1234567813. These unformatted NDCs still serve as unique identifiers for drugs, albeit with a lower confidence level due to the lack of clear segmentation.

Other instances of valid unformatted NDCs are 9876512345, 1234512342, 9876523456, 5432167892, 1234578903, 2345612344, and 8765421233. While these drug identification numbers lack hyphens, they still contain the necessary information for medication identification within the FDA's system.

Invalid unformatted NDCs can be identified by their length or composition. For example, 123456789 is too short, while 123456789012 and 12345678901234 are too long to be valid NDCs. Formatted codes like 1234-5678-90, 12345-678-90, and 12345-6789-0 should not be considered in this category of unformatted codes.

Other examples of invalid unformatted NDCs include non-numeric strings such as abcdefghij, or combinations of numbers and letters like 123456789a and 1234567890a. These do not conform to the FDA's standards for drug identification numbers and should not be recognized as valid medication identification codes in any system.
